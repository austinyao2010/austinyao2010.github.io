  • Community Service

    I initiated a free “Swim with a Splash” event for my community because I had drastically improved in swimming, and I wanted to help other kids become better too. There were many details I had to coordinate. I recruited a few peers to teach and assigned specific roles to avoid conflicts. Using Canva, I created a flyer for our community bulletin board and Facebook and also advertised by word of mouth. Using the information the parents had given to me on a Google form, I made individual lesson plans for each swimmer and assigned them to a coach according to the coach's strengths. We taught eleven kids over three days, confidently giving parents feedback after each lesson. The classes weren't easy, especially since one student was scared of water. It took two days to get him comfortable being in the pool, but he learned how to hold his breath underwater, which was a huge accomplishment. Later, his mom said that he now likes swimming and wants to take more lessons.

  • Stocks

    In gifted class, we played the stock market game, which lasted for three months. At first, my partner and I were cautious.
