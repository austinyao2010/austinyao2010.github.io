Activities

  • Community Service

    • I initiated a free “Swim with a Splash” event for my community where I offered free private lessons to kids.

    • I recruited a few peers to teach, assigned specific roles such as coach and assistant coach, and created a flyer for our community bulletin board, also advertising by social media and word of mouth. Using information from each child's Google registration form, I created individual lesson plans for the swimmers and assigned them to a coach according to the coach's strengths. Safety was a priority, so I assigned two coaches per child.

    • Many times, we had to adapt to unexpected situations. One day, it started raining. I had planned for it to be outdoors at the neighborhood pool, but due to the bad weather, I moved it to the community center. I had to notify all the parents, and we kept going.

    • We had to adjust again as our youngest student, who was just three years old, was hesitant to get into the pool. I had one of our assistant coach's younger brother lead him into the water and assigned an assistant coach to supervise them both. The boy ended up loving the lesson so much that he was willing to jump into the deeper pool. When his mom saw how comfortable he was, she immediately signed up her older child for a lesson too!

    • Surprisingly, we had an adult sign up for a lesson. At first, she only wanted to have one class, but after we gave her professional feedback about her kicking and pulling, she happily had three classes.

    • We taught eleven individuals over three days, confidently giving parents feedback after each lesson. The classes weren't easy, especially since the students were all at different levels. To make one child feel comfortable being in the pool, I had to hold him the entire time he kicked. He was pretty heavy, and I struggled to lift my arms the next day. Finally, after two days of hard work, he was able to hold his breath underwater, which was a huge accomplishment. Later, his mom said that he now likes swimming and wants to take more lessons.

    • Having positive impacts like these have motivated me to do even more to help my community.
