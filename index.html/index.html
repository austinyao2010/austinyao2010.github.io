Community Service

Swim with a Splash: I held a free “Swim with a Splash” event for my community because I had drastically improved in swimming, and I wanted to help other kids become better too. As I organized this class, I developed the new skill of project planning. There were many details I had to coordinate. I recruited a few peers to teach and assigned specific roles to avoid conflicts. Using Canva, I created a flyer for our community bulletin board and Facebook and also advertised by word of mouth. Using the information the parents had given to me on a Google form, I made individual lesson plans for each swimmer and assigned them to a coach according to the coach's strengths. We taught eleven kids over three days, confidently giving parents feedback after each lesson. The classes weren't easy, especially since one student was scared of water. It took two days to get him comfortable being in the pool, but he learned how to hold his breath underwater, which was a huge accomplishment. Later, his mom said that he now likes swimming and wants to take more lessons. Project planning requires a lot of thought and hard work, but the end result is really satisfying. International Food Drive: I was a part of a food drive for Cincinnati AACO Youth Member (Asian-American Coalition of Ohio Youth Chapter) International Food Drive for the University of Cincinnati's Bearcats Pantry, where I was able to gather money and food items for families of graduate students in need.

Surprisingly, with only two people, we placed 2nd out of all our school teams. This experience was fascinating and made me feel confident about investing in the future. This prompted me to actually invest in real life by asking my dad to invest the money in the stocks I asked him to, and I've already earned over 13% of the stock's value.
